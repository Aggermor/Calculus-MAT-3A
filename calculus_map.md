<div class="big-picture">

Calculus starts by asking: **How does something change?**  
Then it builds tools to measure change, predict behavior, optimize situations, and measure accumulated total amounts.

**Limits → Derivatives → Derivative Applications → Integrals → Integral Applications**

Derivatives measure change. Integrals measure accumulation. The first half of the course builds the derivative as a tool for slopes, rates, graph behavior, and optimization. The second half builds the integral as a tool for area, total change, area between curves, and volume.

## Whole-course summary

**Derivatives measure change.** They answer questions like: How fast is something changing? Where is it increasing or decreasing? Where is it biggest or smallest? What does the graph look like?

**Integrals measure accumulation.** They answer questions like: How much has accumulated? What is the total area? What is the total change over an interval? How can area be turned into volume?
